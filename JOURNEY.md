# Mighty Mitochondria — Experience & Content Spec

*A scroll-driven 3D journey that teaches mitochondrial structure and function. Lock this before building.*

*Revision 2, 2026-07-03: incorporates the science-verification pass. Changes are flagged inline with "REVISED" notes so the reasoning is traceable. Every factual claim here traces to RESEARCH.md.*

---

## 1. Core experience principles

- **Guided, not free-roam.** The camera is scroll-linked. Scrolling drives a fixed cinematic path (a "rail"), so no one gets lost inside a 3D blob. Scroll down = forward through the story; scroll up = rewind.
- **One idea per stop.** Each scene delivers a single concept in 2-4 sentences. No walls of text floating in 3D space.
- **Earn the depth.** Quiz gates block forward progress until answered correctly, then reward with a "dive deeper" transition (the spiral effect).
- **Settled first, then the frontier, and say so.** The ride teaches known textbook biology first, then crosses a clearly marked threshold into active research (aging, exercise, disease). The visitor is told, on screen, when we leave settled ground. This builds trust and teaches the difference between "known" and "still being worked out."
- **Captivating but not busy.** Glow, particle flow, and smooth easing, yes. Constant flashing and chaos, no. The motion should feel like a slow, confident roller coaster, not a screensaver.
- **The payoff shot is ATP synthase.** Everything builds toward the spinning molecular motor that stamps out energy. Design the whole ride so that moment lands as the visual climax.

## 2. The journey, scene by scene

### Scene 0 — Cold open / title
- **Visual:** Black. A single distant point of golden light drifts in dark cytoplasm, slowly resolving into a bean-shaped mitochondrion, semi-translucent so the inner folds glow faintly through the surface.
- **Camera:** Static, slow drift. Gentle rotation of the organelle.
- **Copy:** "Mighty Mitochondria." Subtitle: *The tiny engine that powers almost everything you do.* Prompt: *Scroll to begin the descent.*
- **Effect:** Soft bloom, floating dust particles, subtle depth of field.

### Scene 1 — Approach & overview
- **Visual:** Camera descends toward the mitochondrion. It rotates slowly, revealing its overall shape and a hint of the folded interior.
- **Camera:** Smooth dolly-in from far to medium distance.
- **Copy:** *This is a mitochondrion, an organelle inside almost every cell in your body. It has one famous job: it doesn't make energy from nothing, it unlocks the energy already stored in the food you've eaten and repackages it into a form your cells can actually spend. You carry an astonishing number of them: at least hundreds in a busy cell, tens of trillions of cells in you, which stacks up into quadrillions. Nobody has an exact count. Sabotage them and you have minutes, not hours: that is exactly how cyanide kills, by jamming this machine so the body simply runs out of power.*
- **Effect:** Gentle rotation, particle drift, warm interior glow pulsing like a heartbeat.
- **REVISED (three fixes):** (1) "turning food and oxygen into usable energy" became "unlocks... and repackages," so the copy makes clear energy is extracted by a chemical process, not created. (2) "You have trillions of them" became the floor-framed "at least... at least... quadrillions, no exact count," because there is no rigorous total and the honest version is a stronger hook. (3) "seconds to live" became "minutes, not hours," with the cyanide anchor, because minutes is the accurate figure and cyanide makes it more dramatic while being true. See RESEARCH.md.

### Scene 2 — The outer membrane (the gateway)
- **Visual:** Camera moves to the surface. The smooth outer membrane, dotted with porin channels, fills the frame.
- **Camera:** Push toward the surface, slow parallax over the membrane.
- **Copy:** *First, the outer wall. It's a border, but a relaxed one, studded with pores that let small molecules drift in and out freely. Think checkpoint, not fortress.*
- **Effect:** Small molecules float through the porins as glowing specks.

### Scene 3 — Inner membrane & cristae (the folds)
- **Visual:** Camera slips through the outer membrane into the narrow intermembrane space, then the dramatically folded inner membrane (cristae) is revealed, wall after wall of folds.
- **Camera:** Pass-through transition, then a sweeping reveal along the folds.
- **Copy:** *Slip inside and everything changes. The inner membrane is packed into deep folds called cristae. The trick is surface area: more folds mean more room for the machinery that makes energy. And unlike the outer wall, this one is sealed tight. Remember that seal. It's the whole reason the next part works.*
- **Effect:** The folds catch light as the camera sweeps; faint energy shimmer along the membrane surface.
- **REVISED (small):** added the "remember that seal" line to plant the setup for Scene 5, where the sealed membrane is what lets a charge build.

### Scene 4 — The matrix (the engine room)
- **Visual:** Spiral through the opened membrane into the matrix, the gooey innermost space. Floating enzymes, a loop of DNA, tiny ribosomes.
- **Camera:** Spiral-in, then settle into a slow float through the interior.
- **Copy:** *This inner space is the matrix, and it hides a secret: the mitochondrion has its own DNA, separate from the rest of the cell. A clue we'll come back to. An earlier step of energy extraction already happened out in the cell, before anything reached here. What happens inside the mitochondrion is the next stage: a loop of chemistry called the citric acid cycle breaks the fuel down further and loads up tiny "electron carriers" for the main event.*
- **Effect:** Floating particle enzymes, a glowing circular strand of mtDNA, warm ambient haze.
- **REVISED (load-bearing fix):** the old copy called this "where the first stage of energy extraction happens." That is wrong: the genuine first stage is glycolysis, in the cytoplasm, outside the mitochondrion. The matrix runs the citric acid cycle (the next stage). Copy now says an earlier step already happened out in the cell and names the citric acid cycle. See RESEARCH.md.

### Scene 5 — Electron transport chain (charging the battery)
- **Visual:** Camera moves to the inner membrane surface from the inside. A row of protein complexes (stations) is embedded in it. Electrons visibly hop from station to station; at three of the stations, each hop kicks glowing protons across the sealed membrane to the other side. As protons pile up on the far side, a charge builds: the membrane begins to glow with stored tension, and a subtle "charge meter" climbs. Then the camera swings dramatically to one station that behaves differently (Complex II): an electron slides in from a side entrance, coming from the matrix rather than down the main line, and passes straight through without kicking any proton across. Nothing gets pumped at that station.
- **Camera:** Track along the membrane following the electron flow, then a deliberate swing-and-hold on the odd-one-out station.
- **Copy:** *Now the main event. Those electron carriers dump their cargo into a chain of protein stations. As electrons hop down the chain, most stations use the energy to shove protons across the sealed membrane, one side filling up while the other empties. But here's the catch that trips people: protons aren't water. Each one is a naked positive charge. So you're not just piling stuff up, you're peeling positive away from negative and building a voltage. The sealed membrane is charging like a battery. And watch this one station: an electron slips in a side door and passes right through without pumping anything. Fuel that enters here drives less energy than fuel that takes the main road. Not every step pulls the same weight.*
- **Effect:** Directional particle flow; three stations visibly pumping; building density and glow of protons on one side; a rising "charge" shimmer across the membrane; a spotlight beat on the non-pumping station where the electron takes a different path.
- **REVISED (the battery reframe + the Complex II moment):** the "dam / water piling up" metaphor is replaced by "charging a battery." This is not just for flair: the dam captures only the pile-up (the concentration part) and is silent about the voltage (the electrical part), and the voltage is the larger share of the stored energy. Battery captures both, and it sets up Scene 6 as the discharge. The Complex II beat (one station passes electrons without pumping, via a side entrance from the citric acid cycle, which is why that route yields less ATP) is accurate and is the "odd one out" moment requested. Animation guardrail: Complex II must receive its electron from the matrix side, not from the previous station, because it genuinely bypasses Complex I. See RESEARCH.md.

### Scene 6 — ATP synthase (the discharge, the climax)
- **Visual:** THE money shot. The charged membrane has exactly one opening in it, and that opening is ATP synthase, a rotary molecular motor. The pent-up protons rush back through the only door, and their rush physically spins it. It is a revolving gate driven by a crowd. Floating in the matrix are loose parts, ADP and phosphate. Every third of a turn, the spinning motor snaps one ADP and one phosphate together and flings out a finished, glowing gold coin: one ATP. The energy currency is literally being minted.
- **Camera:** Slow orbit around the spinning motor, then a hero push-in as each ATP coin pops out.
- **Copy:** *Here's the payoff. All that stored charge has one way out that makes ATP: back through a single machine, ATP synthase. The rush of protons spins it like a revolving door, and every turn snaps loose parts together into ATP, your body's energy currency. It isn't conjured from nothing; the machine bolts together pieces already floating nearby. You mint roughly your own body weight in ATP every single day, not by making that much new stuff, but by recycling a small amount through this motor hundreds of times over.*
- **Effect:** Spinning geometry; rhythmic pulses of gold ATP coins; peak glow and bloom. This is the visual peak of the whole site. ADP and phosphate must be visible going in so the ATP is clearly assembled, not summoned.
- **REVISED (the mint reframe + one accuracy guardrail + one honesty upgrade):** "turbine" became a "revolving gate that mints coins," which pays off the existing "energy currency" language and keeps the mechanism honest (ATP synthase genuinely rotates, and its head has three catalytic sites, so roughly three ATP per full turn maps onto "every third of a turn, a coin"). Guardrail: ADP and phosphate go in, ATP comes out; nothing is created from nothing, or we would be teaching "energy from nothing," the one idea this site exists to prevent. The body-weight line now also carries the recycling truth, which is the more captivating fact. See RESEARCH.md.

#### Side journey: What is ATP
- **Trigger:** within the Scene 6 scroll range, at or just after ATP is first named, the camera swings right to a clear area near the synthase, holds for a short looping animation, then swings back so the minting climax still lands. Additive to Scene 6; the minting climax is unchanged.
- **Copy:** *Quick detour: what is this coin, actually? ATP is your cell's rechargeable energy carrier, a small molecule with three phosphate groups clipped in a row. When something in the cell needs power, it pops off the outer phosphate to release energy the cell puts to work, leaving ADP behind with two. Later, this very machine clips a phosphate back on, turning ADP back into ATP. You never hoard much of it; you spend and recharge the same molecules hundreds of times a day.*
- **Animation:** a single ATP molecule (an adenosine body with three phosphate beads clipped in a row, glowing gold). The outer phosphate detaches, and that release drives a small visible bit of work: a turning gear that switches on a light. The molecule, now ADP with two phosphates, dims. Then a phosphate clips back on and it glows gold again. Loop the charge, spend, recharge cycle two or three times, then the camera swings back.
- **Color grammar:** the ATP molecule glows gold (correct: ATP is the energy currency and gold is the reserved energy color). The detached phosphate and the ADP body keep the SAME colors as the Scene 6 minting animation (phosphate the pale near-neutral, ADP body the muted cool tone) so they read as the same molecules. The gear is neutral structure and the light is white, so neither competes with the gold. Do not call ATP a "battery" (battery stays reserved for the membrane).
- **Accuracy guardrails:** ATP has three phosphates, ADP has two; show the count change. Energy is released when the phosphate is split off; do not animate a glowing "high-energy bond" snapping. Recharge is ADP plus phosphate making ATP, run by this same ATP synthase.
- **Sources:** RESEARCH.md Part A (ATP structure; hydrolysis to ADP plus phosphate; reversible ATP/ADP cycle run by ATP synthase).

### Scene 7 — Zoom out to the body (where the energy goes)
- **Visual:** Pull back fast through the cell, out into living tissue. Land inside a beating heart-muscle cell (or a firing neuron), packed wall-to-wall with mitochondria.
- **Camera:** Rapid pull-back, then settle into the new tissue scene.
- **Copy:** *So where does all that energy go? Everywhere that never stops working. About a quarter of the volume of your heart-muscle cells is mitochondria: a quarter of the machine is dedicated power plant. Your neurons, your muscles, they're all hungry for the ATP you just watched get minted.*
- **Effect:** Rhythmic pulsing to mimic a heartbeat; dozens of small mitochondria glowing in sync.
- **REVISED (number correction):** "about a third mitochondria by volume" became "about a quarter." Direct measurement of human hearts gives roughly 23%. The "a third" figure is real for small fast-hearted mammals, not humans. See RESEARCH.md.

### Scene 8 — The bigger story (settled)
- **Visual:** A constellation of "did you know" beats, camera drifting between glowing nodes, or a slow sequence of reveals.
- **Copy (as short cards):**
  - *An ancient partnership: mitochondria were once free-living bacteria, swallowed by another cell over a billion years ago. That's why they still carry their own DNA.*
  - *You inherited essentially all of yours from your mother, and her mother, and hers: an unbroken maternal line reaching back further than you'd believe. They were never fully yours to begin with, because they started as that ancient bacterial guest.*
  - *They don't just make energy, they also decide when a cell should die, releasing a signal (cytochrome c) that triggers self-destruction to protect the body.*
  - *Beyond ATP: they generate body heat, store calcium, and help build hormones.*
- **Effect:** Nodes light up as the camera passes; calm, wonder-forward pacing.
- **REVISED (Scene 8 inheritance card):** "every single one" became "essentially all," and the card now flows the maternal line into the ancient-bacteria origin (the "never fully yours" segue). The absolute is dropped because rare paternal transmission is reported and debated; the maternal consensus is kept. See RESEARCH.md. Note: everything in Scene 8 is still settled science, which is why it sits before the threshold.

### THRESHOLD — Leaving settled ground
- **Visual:** A distinct visual shift (for example the palette cools or the space opens out) that signals a boundary being crossed. Not a scene with a fact, a doorway.
- **Copy (on screen):** *From here on, we leave the settled textbook and step into what researchers are still working out. Everything before this line is in every biology course. Everything after it is where the science is still pointing, still arguing, still learning. Here's where it's headed.*
- **Why:** the visitor is explicitly told when known biology ends and active research begins. This is a core principle of the ride, not decoration.

### Scene 9 — The frontier (active research)
- **Visual:** Camera drifts through a more open, exploratory space; frontier beats appear as distinct glowing nodes, visually marked as different from the settled section.
- **Copy (as short cards, each clearly framed as active research):**
  - *Aging: worn-down mitochondria are now considered one of the hallmarks of aging, one of several interacting drivers researchers track, not the single cause.*
  - *Exercise: train your endurance, and your body responds by building more mitochondria. Your cells literally retool for the demand you place on them.*
  - *Disease: when this ancient machine carries a fault, it causes real inherited illness. One example, MELAS, is passed down the same maternal line you just learned about, and it strikes the hungriest organs first: brain, heart, muscle. Roughly 1 in 5,000 people lives with a primary mitochondrial disease.*
- **Effect:** Nodes light up as the camera passes; a slightly cooler, more open feel than the settled section, reinforcing that we've crossed a boundary.
- **REVISED (new section):** this is the frontier arc the project planned but had not written. Each card traces to a named peer-reviewed source in RESEARCH.md Part B (Hallmarks of Aging 2023; 2025 exercise meta-analysis; MELAS reviews and prevalence). Copy avoids the "single master switch" overclaim for exercise and the "the cause of aging" overclaim, per the honesty notes in RESEARCH.md.

### Scene 10 — Closing
- **Visual:** Pull all the way back to a whole cell glowing with countless mitochondria, or a full silhouette lighting up.
- **Copy:** *That's the mighty mitochondrion: an ancient bacterial guest that became the engine of complex life, and of you. Now you know what's powering every beat, breath, and thought, and where scientists are still chasing the rest of the story.*
- **Effect:** Final swell of light, then a gentle settle. Optional share button + "restart the journey."
- **REVISED (was Scene 9):** renumbered to 10 after inserting the threshold and frontier section. Closing line now nods to the open questions, matching the settled-then-frontier arc.

## 4. Visual language

- **Palette:** Deep blue/teal base (the dark interior), electric cyan for structure and membranes, warm amber/gold reserved *specifically* for energy (ATP, electron flow). Keeping gold as "the energy color" gives the whole ride a visual grammar. Consider a cooler shift in the frontier section to reinforce the threshold.
- **The unified energy metaphor (Scenes 5 and 6):** charge a battery, then discharge it through a coin-stamping revolving gate. Scene 5 charges the sealed membrane (protons as charge building a voltage, not just water piling up). Scene 6 discharges that charge through the one door, ATP synthase, which spins and mints gold ATP coins. Keep the two true ideas underneath at all costs: a gradient is stored electrochemical energy, and ATP synthase is a real rotary motor. The visual skin is free to be fresh; the mechanism is not.
- **Where the metaphor breaks (so the animation doesn't teach it wrong):** the mint must show ADP and phosphate going in and ATP coming out. ATP is assembled from parts already present, never created from nothing. A coin appearing from thin air would teach "energy from nothing," which is the exact misconception this site exists to correct.
- **Motion:** Everything eased and smooth. Scroll snaps softly to each scene rather than free-floating. Slow rotations, gentle parallax.
- **Post-processing:** Bloom/glow, subtle depth of field, light particle systems. Dial these *up* at ATP synthase, *down* in quieter explanatory scenes.
- **Transitions:** The signature move is the "spiral dive" through a quiz gate into the next depth. Reuse it so it becomes a recognizable reward.

## 5. Open decisions to settle before building

These are the things that'll bite us later if we don't decide now:

1. **Scroll behavior:** Scroll-snap to each scene (recommended, keeps pacing tight) vs free continuous scroll.
2. **Wrong-answer behavior:** Gentle hint that points back to the relevant scene (recommended) vs just blocking.
3. **Progress indicator:** A small journey map or progress bar so users know how far along they are. Recommended, it reduces drop-off. Could visually mark the settled/frontier boundary.
4. **Mobile plan:** 3D is heavy on phones. Decide now: reduced particle counts + simpler geometry on mobile, and a graceful fallback if a device can't handle WebGL.
5. **Accessibility:** A "reduce motion" toggle and a plain-text version of the content. Worth doing, and not much extra work if planned up front.
6. **Audio:** Optional ambient soundscape, muted by default. Nice-to-have, not essential for v1.
7. **Scope for v1:** Do we build all scenes first, or ship 0-6 (through ATP synthase) as a first playable, then add 7-10? Leaning toward building the full skeleton with placeholder art, then polishing scene by scene.
